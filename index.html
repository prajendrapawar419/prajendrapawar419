LLM Benchmarking Framework

At Deutsche Bank I got to do something I found genuinely interesting — we needed to figure out which LLMs were actually worth deploying in a regulated financial environment, and the answer wasn't obvious. So I built an evaluation framework to find out.

The framework compares models on the things that actually matter for enterprise use: TTFT, throughput, output quality, hallucination rate, and cost per token. The finding that shaped the final architecture: GPT-4 had the best accuracy and lowest hallucination rate but cost 15x more than Gemini. The answer wasn't "use the best model" — it was use the right model for the right risk level.

Time-Series Forecasting · Risk Models
Market Risk Forecasting Pipeline

One of the things I worked on at Deutsche Bank was building the forecasting layer for market risk and liquidity models. The pipeline runs daily across 25M+ records from multiple asset class systems.

The interesting part was the model selection decision. ARIMA is interpretable and fast. Prophet handles seasonality well. LSTM captures non-linear patterns but needs more data and compute. The choice isn't about which model is best in theory — it's about which one you can explain to a risk committee and audit under Basel III. That constraint shaped the whole architecture.

NLP · Healthcare · MLOps
Clinical NLP Anomaly Detection

At HCLTech I built a pipeline that analyzed physician notes to catch ICD-10 coding errors before they caused billing problems or compliance issues. Medical coders were spending hours on manual review — the goal was to make that review targeted rather than exhaustive.

The pipeline uses SpaCy for clinical entity extraction and TF-IDF for anomaly scoring. The deployment constraint made this more interesting than the model itself — everything had to be HIPAA-compliant, which meant anonymization before any data touched the model, strict audit logging, and zero PII in model inputs or outputs.

# Anonymize first — no PII ever reaches the model
